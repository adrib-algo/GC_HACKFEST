Hackathon Guidelines

1. Overview

This college-level hackathon is inspired by Smart India Hackathon (SIH) and aims to prepare students for future participation by focusing on innovation, teamwork, problem understanding, and real-world impact. Teams will build a working prototype during the hackathon hours.

2. Event Structure

  • Mode: Offline (George College)
  • Duration: 1 Day
  • Time: 9:00 AM – 4:00 PM
  • Team Size: 6 students (1 female mandatory)
  • Evaluation Rounds: 3

3. Team Formation Rules

  • Each team must have 6 members with at least 1 female member.
  • Cross-year teams are allowed.
  • A student can be part of only one team.
  • No team changes after registration.

4. Problem Statement Rules

  • Problem statements will be released 7 days before the hackathon.
  • Teams must choose only one problem statement.
  • Modification of the core problem statement is not allowed.
  • Solutions must strictly align with the chosen problem.

5. Pre-Hackathon Submission (Mandatory)

  • Problem statement
  • Proposed solution approach
  • Technology stack
  • PPT

Note: Teams failing to submit will not be allowed to participate.

6. Hackathon Day Timeline

9:00 – 9:30 AM | Opening & Briefing

9:30 – 10:00 AM | Evaluation Round 1 (Idea & Architecture)

10:00 – 11:30 AM | Build Phase 1

11:30 – 12:00 PM | Evaluation Round 2 (Functional Review)

12:00 – 12:30 PM | Break

12:30 – 2:30 PM | Build Phase 2

2:30 – 3:30 PM | Evaluation Round 3 (Final Demo & Impact)

7. Evaluation Criteria (Total: 100 Points)

  • Idea & Architecture – 20 Points
  • Functionality – 30 Points
  • Final Product & Impact – 50 Points

Judges’ decisions are final and binding.

8. Technology Rules

  • Any programming language or framework may be used.
  • Open-source libraries and APIs are allowed.

9. Anti-Plagiarism Policy

  • Copying code or cloned projects is strictly prohibited.
  • Teams found guilty will face immediate disqualification.
  • Judges may ask random code walkthrough questions.

10. Code Ownership & Submission

  • Source code must be submitted via Git repository.
  • Final PPT (maximum 7 slides) must be submitted.
  • Submission deadline: 3:00 PM sharp.

11. Awards & Recognition

  • Winner Team
  • All participants will receive certificates.

12. Code of Conduct

  • Maintain professionalism and discipline.
  • Respect judges, volunteers, and fellow participants.
  • Any misconduct may lead to disqualification.
